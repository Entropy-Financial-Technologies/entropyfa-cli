# Afr 2026 03

## What This Is

IRS Applicable Federal Rates (AFR) for March 2026 as published in Rev. Rul. 2026-6 (IRB 2026-11). Contains 12 rate values: 3 term categories (short-term, mid-term, long-term) × 4 compounding periods (annual, semiannual, quarterly, monthly), all at the base 100% AFR level prescribed under IRC §1274(d).

## Lookup Parameters

- term_category: one of short_term, mid_term, or long_term — determined by the maturity of the debt instrument or loan
- compounding_period: one of annual, semiannual, quarterly, or monthly — determined by the payment frequency specified in the loan or instrument

## Interpretation Notes

- Values are percentages as published (e.g. 3.59 means 3.59%, not 0.0359).
- Short-term: instruments with maturity of 3 years or less. Mid-term: over 3 years up to 9 years. Long-term: over 9 years.
- Compounding period variants (annual, semiannual, quarterly, monthly) reflect the equivalent yield for that compounding frequency. The annual rate is the highest; monthly is the lowest for any given term.
- The AFR is a minimum safe-harbor interest rate; loans at or above the AFR for the applicable term and compounding avoid imputed-interest rules under IRC §§1274, 7872, and related provisions.

## Does Not Include

- Adjusted AFR rates for tax-exempt obligations under IRC §1288(b) (Table 2 of the revenue ruling)
- Section 382 adjusted federal long-term rate and long-term tax-exempt rate (Table 3)
- Section 42(b)(1) low-income housing credit percentages (Table 4)
- Section 7520 rate for valuing annuities, life estates, and remainders (Table 5)
- 110%, 120%, 130%, 150%, or 175% multiples of the AFR

## Caveats

- AFR rates change monthly; these rates apply only to March 2026. Do not use for other months.
- This dataset contains only the base AFR rates (100%). The IRS also publishes 110%, 120%, 130%, 150%, and 175% AFR rates in the same revenue ruling; those are not included here.
- Rates are derived from average market yields on outstanding marketable obligations of the United States during the monthly period; the IRS methodology is prescribed by IRC §1274(d) and is not user-adjustable.

## Typical Uses

- Setting minimum interest rates on intra-family loans to avoid gift-tax consequences under IRC §7872
- Determining original issue discount (OID) on debt instruments issued for property under IRC §1274
- Establishing safe-harbor interest rates for seller-financed installment sales under IRC §483
