# Afr 2026 01

## What This Is

IRS Applicable Federal Rates (AFR) for January 2026, published in Revenue Ruling 2026-2. Contains 12 values: the base AFR for each of 3 term categories (short-term, mid-term, long-term) across 4 compounding periods (annual, semiannual, quarterly, monthly), as prescribed under IRC §1274(d).

## Lookup Parameters

- term_category: one of short_term, mid_term, or long_term — selected based on the weighted average maturity of the debt instrument
- compounding_period: one of annual, semiannual, quarterly, or monthly — selected based on the payment frequency stated in the instrument

## Interpretation Notes

- Values are percentages as published (e.g. 3.63 means 3.63%, not 0.0363).
- Short-term applies to debt instruments with maturities of 3 years or less; mid-term covers over 3 years through 9 years; long-term covers over 9 years.
- The four compounding periods (annual, semiannual, quarterly, monthly) are mathematically equivalent effective rates; select the period matching the debt instrument's stated payment frequency.
- Rates are determined from a one-month average of market yields on marketable obligations of the United States.

## Does Not Include

- Adjusted AFR rates (Table 2 of Rev. Rul. 2026-2, used for tax-exempt obligations under IRC §1288(b))
- Section 382 adjusted federal long-term rate or long-term tax-exempt rate (Table 3)
- Section 7520 rate for valuing annuities, life estates, and remainders (Table 5)
- 110%, 120%, 130%, 150%, or 175% AFR multiples used for safe-harbor and penalty thresholds

## Caveats

- AFRs change monthly; these rates apply only to January 2026. Transactions closing in a different month must use that month's published rates.
- This dataset contains only the base AFR rows (100% AFR). It does not include the 110%, 120%, 130%, 150%, or 175% AFR multiples also published in Rev. Rul. 2026-2 Table 1.
- AFRs are minimum rates for federal tax purposes and do not represent market lending rates or recommended interest rates.

## Typical Uses

- Setting the minimum interest rate on intra-family loans to avoid imputed income under IRC §7872
- Determining original issue discount (OID) and unstated interest on seller-financed sales under IRC §§1274 and 483
- Establishing adequate stated interest for debt-for-property exchanges
