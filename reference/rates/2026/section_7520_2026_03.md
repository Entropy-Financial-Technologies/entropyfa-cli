# Section 7520 2026 03

## What This Is

The IRC §7520 interest rate for March 2026, published by the IRS in Rev. Rul. 2026-6. This rate (4.8%) equals 120% of the applicable federal mid-term rate (annual compounding) of 4.72%, rounded to the nearest two-tenths of one percent. It is the discount rate used with IRS actuarial tables to value annuities, life estates, remainders, and reversions for federal estate, gift, and income tax purposes.

## Lookup Parameters

- No variant parameters — there is one rate per month, stored under the 'default' variant.

## Interpretation Notes

- The rate is expressed as a percentage: 4.8 means 4.8% per annum.
- This is a single scalar value with no variant parameters (filing_status and lived_with_spouse_during_year are null).
- The rate is used as the discount/accumulation rate in IRS actuarial tables for valuing annuities, life estates, remainders, and reversions under IRC §7520.

## Does Not Include

- Applicable Federal Rates (short-term, mid-term, long-term AFR) — those are separate datasets.
- Section 7520 actuarial tables (mortality component) — only the interest rate component is stored here.
- Section 7520 rates for months other than March 2026.

## Caveats

- The §7520 rate changes monthly; this entry covers March 2026 only.
- The rate is derived from 120% of the mid-term AFR (annual compounding), rounded to the nearest 0.2%. Rounding can cause the §7520 rate to stay flat even when the underlying AFR shifts slightly.
- Taxpayers may elect to use the §7520 rate from one of the two months preceding the valuation date (IRC §7520(a)), so a March 2026 valuation could use the January 2026 or February 2026 rate instead.

## Typical Uses

- Valuing charitable remainder trusts (CRTs) and charitable lead trusts (CLTs) for gift and estate tax purposes.
- Valuing retained interests in grantor retained annuity trusts (GRATs).
- Valuing life estates and remainder interests for estate and gift tax returns.
