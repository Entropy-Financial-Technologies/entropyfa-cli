# Afr 2026 02

## What This Is

IRS Applicable Federal Rates (AFR) for February 2026, published in Rev. Rul. 2026-3 (IRB 2026-06). Contains 12 values: the 100% AFR for three term categories (short-term, mid-term, long-term) at four compounding periods (annual, semiannual, quarterly, monthly), prescribed under IRC §1274(d).

## Lookup Parameters

- term_category: one of short_term, mid_term, or long_term, determined by the weighted average maturity of the debt instrument
- compounding_period: one of annual, semiannual, quarterly, or monthly, matching the instrument's stated compounding interval

## Interpretation Notes

- Values are percentages as published (e.g., 4.70 means 4.70%, not 0.0470).
- Term categories: short-term (≤3 years), mid-term (>3 years and ≤9 years), long-term (>9 years).
- Compounding periods: annual, semiannual, quarterly, monthly. Lower compounding frequency yields a slightly higher equivalent annual rate.
- The applicable rate depends on the compounding period specified in the debt instrument. If no compounding period is stated, semiannual compounding is assumed under IRC §1274(d).

## Does Not Include

- Adjusted AFR rates under IRC §1288(b) (Table 2 of the revenue ruling)
- Adjusted federal long-term rate and long-term tax-exempt rate under IRC §382(f) (Table 3)
- Low-income housing credit percentages under IRC §42(b)(1) (Table 4)
- Section 7520 rate for February 2026
- 110%, 120%, 130%, 150%, or 175% AFR multiples

## Caveats

- AFR rates change monthly; these values apply only to February 2026 transactions.
- This dataset contains only the 100% AFR rates. The IRS also publishes 110%, 120%, 130%, 150%, and 175% AFR rates in the same revenue ruling; those are not included here.
- AFR rates are determined from average market yields on outstanding marketable obligations of the United States during the one-month period ending in the month prior to publication.

## Typical Uses

- Minimum interest rate for below-market loans under IRC §7872
- Testing for adequate stated interest and original issue discount (OID) under IRC §1274
- Intra-family loan rate benchmarking for estate and gift tax purposes
- Safe-harbor interest rates for seller-financed transactions
